Physics 399: Senior Seminar
Department of Physics, Haverford College
Fall 2004

Welcome

Welcome to the senior seminar. In this course you will participate in the following:
  • Practice writing a scientific paper
  • Learn how to give a scientific presentation
  • Hear lectures by eminent scientists and interact with them
  • Learn more about careers available to you when you are finished at Haverford
  • Get into contact with alumni who have gone in career directions of interest to you
  • Learn about graduate school and how to go about selecting an applying to schools
  • Discuss topics relevant to science
The class meets on many Wednesdays throughout the semester, but with several other special events. Copies of all assigned readings in the course will be made available to you in advance of the class meeting. You are expected to have read the assignment and to come prepared to discuss it. A binder in the physics lounge contains reports on some alumni who were contacted by senior seminarians in previous years.

Course Requirements (Fall)

  • Attend all scheduled meetings of the seminar, including any added later. (You will be informed by announcement or email if we add any sessions.)
  • Attend Philips visits and department colloquia. We hope you will also attend the informal meetings with our visitors. They enjoy talking with students and these informal gatherings are a great way to find out about the speaker and what he or she is up to.
  • Read assigned selections and participate in class discussions.
  • Contact two Haverford physics grads who are in non-academic jobs (you may include no more than one graduate student), discuss their careers with them and prepare a one-page report and a brief (5-10 min.) presentation for the rest of the class. Reports will be graded only on a credit/no credit basis, but hand them in on the date of the presentations for full credit. Also keep in mind that they will go into the binder, so following cohorts of physics majors will be reading them. You should begin the process of selection and contact well in advance of the presentation date (that is, as soon as possible).
  • Write a short paper (4-5 pages) consisting of a preliminary version of the introduction and background sections of your senior paper, plus a short (one page) description of your project as a whole. This is due at the end of the semester and will be the basis of a brief (10 min.) informal presentation that you will give to the seminar.

Grading

You will receive one grade at the end of the academic year for PHYS 399 as a whole. (The grade of CIP, course in progress, will be submitted at the end of the fall semester.) The physics faculty working together assigns grades in the senior seminar according to the following weighting factors:
  • Fall semester short paper (10%)
  • Fall semester oral presentations/class participation (20%)
  • Quality of senior talk (30%)
  • Senior paper (spring semester) (40%)
A reduction of 0.1 grade points will be applied per unexcused absence if more than one occurs per semester. (This includes attendance at required colloquia and talks.)
