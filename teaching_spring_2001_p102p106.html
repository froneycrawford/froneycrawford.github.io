Class Policies and Guidelines

  • Lab Meeting Times: Alternate weeks (Tuesday or Wednesday) from 1:15 PM to 4:00 PM (see schedule below)
  • Lab Meeting Place: Stokes 108b

  • The labs will be conducted in pairs, but the lab write-up will be done individually.
  • If you are unable to attend a lab, contact one of the instructors in advance to arrange to make it up.
  • Be sure to bring to lab:
    • a pen
    • a calculator
    • paper (loose leaf to keep notes on)
  • Read up on the lab in the manual prior to coming to lab.
  • Prepare written answers to the pre-lab questions in the manual and hand them in when you arrive at lab.
  • For all but one of the experiments, use the lab report forms at the back of the manual to write up the experiment (each of these labs is worth 20 points).
  • For one of the experiments, you are required to write up a formal lab report (see the lab manual for details). This lab is worth 40 points.
  • Lab reports are to be turned in during class on the Monday following the lab.
  • Students in P106 will have a Practical Exam at the end of the term. You will be asked to set up an experiment, take data, plot a graph, and extract useful information from the graph. The instructors will also ask you a few questions to make sure you understand the experiment. This exam will be performed individually and is worth 40 points (see the lab manual for details).
  • Safety: if there is any question or uncertainty about a safety issue, ask the instructors!. This is important; we don't want you to get hurt.
